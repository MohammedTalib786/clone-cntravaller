Alibaug is as synonymous with golden beaches as it is with sprawling villas. But what many don't realise is that these stunning hideouts don't always have to cost as grand as they look. Here are 18 villas as gorgeous as the town itself that you can rent without burning a hole in your pocket:


Amolee

This heritage home in Alibaug features a gorgeous backyard. Over 200 years old, the pet-friendly villa is a masterpiece of teakwood, brick and mortar. It features a dining room and a reading room. There’s also a massive balcony on the first floor, and a swimming pool with an outdoor bar on the ground floor. The chef on the property will cook you Konkani fare or set up a barbeque by the pool. Play badminton on the lawn, sip tea in your balcony or get competitive over a round of board games.

Aqua Villa


This ivory villa comes with three rooms and ensuite bathrooms. Spend evenings strolling in the lawns here or take a dip in the pool. Inside, the villa is tastefully curated with furnishings in muted tones, with paintings on the walls adding a touch of colour to the space. There’s a caretaker on the property to help you out during your stay, and the kitchen comes kitted with appliances for basic cooking and reheating of meals.

What does it mean to you to be an Indian woman in a globalised world?

As much as my roots mean a lot to me, it’s hard to quantify what it means to be an Indian woman in a globalised world because I also feel like I am other things. I don’t generally label myself as this or that because I think those boxes sometimes limit us. I am Indian but I am also other things.


Breeze by the Beach

Beach babies are going to love this villa that’s just five minutes from the jetty. It’s where guests can lounge in the shade of coconut trees and enjoy their private pocket of a sun-kissed beach. There’s a pool, verandah, hammocks and a cabana by the sea. Add to that the 1,000sqft lawn, and you’ve got yourself a breezy beach villa perfect for unwinding amidst nature. The first floor features a common balcony from which you can take in the view of your surroundings while the little ones snooze on the hammock or keep busy on the swing. Food can be prepared in-house, or ordered from local eateries.

Mango Beach House

A 15-minute drive from Mandwa Jetty and a stone's throw away from Kihim Beach, Mango Beach House offers a quick escape from the bustle of the city. Its five villas can accommodate four to sixteen people depending on which one you pick. Each unit boasts a unique personality with varied interiors, layouts and characteristics: while Mango Home is a restored local home with an old-world charm, Casa Mango features a romantic verandah crawling with shrubbery. All villas come with a private pool and facilities such as beach access, concierge service and local Konkani cuisine from the in-house restaurant.



The Valdivian Villa

This beautiful three-bedroom villa sits in a pocket of greenery surrounded by fruit trees. An elevated yoga deck featuring a large Shiva statue looks out to the hills beyond. There’s also a treehouse that welcomes children and adults alike. There are swings and daybeds dotting the outdoor area for a day of casual sunbathing. Inside, colonial furnishings decorate the space. Guests can set up a barbeque and bonfire, or play a game of carrom, chess, volleyball, basketball and darts. The kitchen is out of bounds, but you can place your orders with the cook. Meat is not allowed on the property.


What has been the biggest surprise and challenge on your journey as a global Indian artiste?

I suppose the biggest surprise is purely that I've been able to make music that feels true to what my heart desires and that there is a listenership for it. That really blows me away. When I meet people who come up to me and say what my music means to them, that is a surprise to me in a beautiful way every time… I suppose the biggest challenge continues to be working against my own self-doubts and my own inner critic. That is so inhibiting to creativity. When I’m on my own, left alone to make music, the voice in my head says I've got nothing to say. And so I’m learning to trust the creative process and learning to lovingly put aside that negative voice. I'm not always very good at it and that is something that I continue to try and grow through.


What always makes you feel at home when you’re travelling?

I'm a real fan of creature comforts. I've been known to even travel with a favourite mug when I'm travelling for a long time. There's something comfortable but also soothing and ritualistic about something like having a warm beautiful mug to cut my hands around in the morning or in the evening. To connect with myself, I travel with a journal. Maybe sometimes, a favourite shawl that I may drape in a hotel room.


How would you define your sense of style and how much has it been influenced by your travels?

I'm quite seasonal. I lean into a kind of hippie reference. Very flowing. Feminine style in spring and summer, whereas in autumn and winter, I really like a hard edge and lots of black and sharp lines. But I also like to mix things up a little bit. I might put on a very Indian shawl with those really hard lines or I might wear my biker boots in the summer. I like throwing in a little something that gives a little contrast and difference.


Greystone Manor

The minimalist stone-and-glass interiors of this villa contrast the abundant greenery surrounding it. Large glass panels look out to the natural beauty, and there are open spaces, courtyards and an outdoor bar for you to lounge in. The villa is adorned with family heirlooms standing out vibrantly against the charcoal walls inside and fountains that line the entry foyer outside. There are four attendants to serve you food from the all-vegetarian menu, and you can also order food from outside. The piece de resistance of this home is its beautiful swimming pool that sits in the centre.


Praanam

This chic villa boasts a large round-shaped pool out front. With a gazebo, poolside loungers and a garden adjacent to the house, you’ll be hard-pressed to spend your time indoors. But walk inside, and you’ll find all creature comforts, from elevators and indoor games to a sleek island bar. There are three rooms with attached balconies, and all six of them have ensuite bathrooms. For larger parties, you can choose to cosy up in either or both of the living rooms, the dining room or the outdoor lawn with its rattan furniture. This is where your kids can also hang out on the trampoline and swings. You can have food cooked from the vegetarian menu, or order something from outside. The team will also set up a barbecue for you if you like.
