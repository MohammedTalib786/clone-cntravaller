I’m in the heart of Anjuna in peak season and there’s not a tourist (or anyone else in sight). In today’s Goa, can there be a truer luxury?

My oasis of quiet is The Orchard Manor, a 12,000-sq ft, six-bedroom mansion that holds within it an eclectic mix of Art Deco furniture, auction house pieces, curated art, and the promise of a good vacation.


Orchard Manor, Anjuna

My first thought on entering is how beautifully it embraces openness, welcoming natural light from all sides. The hall has a high ceiling and meanders around a dining area, kitchen, and bedrooms. Outside, a porch skirts the house, overlooking a small lawn and a pool. There are six bedrooms, equal in size but unique in design, decor, and art. The two rooms on the ground floor have easy access to the garden and feature indoor and outdoor baths. Upstairs, accessed by a wooden staircase, are three rooms with enclosed balconies and a long, shared verandah. Another flight of stairs leads to the penthouse, which has its own outdoor balcony.

The Orchard Manor is the pride of Ozzy Serrao, an ex-merchant navy captain. The property started life as his home in 2018. But as it grew, it found a new purpose. Every bit of décor or furniture in the house is something Serrao has collected and they all have a story. My hands graze over the ivory of the Blüthner grand piano in the hall, brought in 1874 by Irish nuns, that’s taught students for 100 years. There’s a lamp that survived the attack at the Taj Mahal Palace in Mumbai; Chinese Martaban stone jars that once transported pickles, now serving as plant pots; and a framed copy of the first floor plan of the Goa Medical College building picked up from a raddiwala. I spend my first half hour there just admiring the lights—each piece is unique, and in the evenings, bathes the house in a warm glow.


At Orchard Manor, I realise that the beauty of the place is in its details. It’s in the little touches that Serrao and his friend, designer Paul Rodrigues, have put around the place, making it a lovely, unexpected treasure hunt. There’s an abundance of Burma teak cupboards, mahogany and rosewood, and Dutch colonial blanket chests. I am thrilled to find three libraries packed with old collections sourced from Europe. There’s art everywhere, even in the bathrooms. I am particularly captivated by a dressed Raja Ravi Varma oleograph.

Sleep comes easily at The Orchard Manor. Morning is lovely, even if I miss the sunrise. There’s a stillness broken by the sounds of birds. There’s a birding corner on the first floor, with binoculars and a book on birds, but I skip that to sit in the garden and watch them fly around me.

The house comes with two chefs and the food is as fresh as it gets: fish brought fresh daily and vegetables sourced from a hydroponic farm nearby; the kitchen has its own herb garden. There’s only one meal offered with the stay besides breakfast—floating on a pool, if you please—because the chefs do everything from scratch. I am fortunate to have two meals and they are restaurant-worthy but with that homely touch. The Goan meal has jumbo prawn balchao, chicken cafreal, red snapper with a fiery recheado masala, and mushroom xacuti. Lunch, the next day, is a lavish Italian spread.

Serrao believes The Orchard Manor’s true value lies in its personalised experience. There is a staff of 15 on hand to cater to my every whim. Serrao himself handles the complimentary concierge service, helping create a full itinerary, either outside or at the mansion. While he promises the best tables at Goa’s leading clubs and restaurants, I am content to stay in. In the evening, I sip whisky at the bar and then go for a swim. A morning of yoga by the pool is followed by a one-hour deep-tissue massage that melts my aches away. For a touch of whimsy, Serrao brings in a tarot card reader. Even though I don’t believe in it, much of what she says is true and the session ends up being a lesson in the art of tarot. I spend the rest of the day sitting at different seating spots, watching for birds, and enjoying the quiet.


The Orchard Manor, Anjuna Mapusa Road, Gaunvaddi, Anjuna, Goa. Entire villa from Rs1.5 lakh + taxes; Call 9819046730.
